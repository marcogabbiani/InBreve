#5

 

The Anti-Reality

If several teams of scientists and entire laboratories have been wondering about anti-matter for years, why should designers not be able to study anti-reality too? Well, not only do they study it, but they also imagine and visualize it too.

The result is this beautiful account (which comes from one of your suggestions) and which tells us of one, or rather, of many beautiful realities to see and - who knows - maybe one day visit.


#4


Read to me

In this masterpiece film by Truffaut, with this masterpiece title sequence, taken from this masterpiece book by Bradbury, there is a world in which firemen, instead of putting out fires, start them. To burn books, to be exact, which are considered illegal by the system.

[SPOILER ALERT: skip the following sentence, if you want to watch the movie or to read the book or both, and you don't want to know how they end]

But humankind is able to adapt and survive even in the worst conditions and so, every person in this world (we hope only dystopian) is assigned the task of memorizing one and only one book, so that they can transmit it to others orally, since that’s the only way to keep the words and meanings inside them safe. Recovering the ancient rhapsodic tradition previous to the invention of writing.

This wonderful initiative of the Italian Union of the Blind and Visually Impaired (that some of you pointed out to me, and I am grateful) reminds a bit of a Bradbury plot: you can read and record a book and make your voice available to a blind person who will be able to listen to it. In short, you can read it for them and of course, a little for yourself. 

Just a shame that the site design does not do justice to the beauty of the project. But sometimes the pulp is worth more than the peel.


#3

 

Can the youngest social media be Vintage?

TikTok is still a very polarizing platform. Heavy users on the one hand, boomer detractors on the other.

However, more and more boomers - or at least millennials - are on TikTok, and it's not hard to imagine that what happened to Facebook will happen to this social media sensation: the older people will make young digital nomads run away, recovering their place somewhere else. No teen wants to post videos on a platform where they risk running into their math professor. Therefore, while the platform continues to define its raison d'etre, in the meantime some creators have found a very interesting editorial line.


I really liked the profile of Jasmine Chiswell, focused on the search for vintage style. There are several nice contents in her feed and also some nice ways to use a young platform with content that looks to the past. Although, to quote the very Marilyn that Jasmine is inspired by, "nostalgia is unglamorous".


#2

 

Virtual fashion instagram show

Riddled by the blows of a painful history, the beauty of the Congo is often untapped and neglected. Only in a few but brilliant cases did advertising approach this world. But where marketing has not arrived, fashion has.

The kindness, beauty, composure, majesty, strength, power and hope of the Congolese spirit have in fact inspired this collection, which for the first time is shown only on Instagram.

"During the creation of each piece", says the designer, "the stories that my mother told me about the women she knew in the Congo came to my mind. I hope this collection will inspire all women to live their power with their heads held high, just like the Democratic Republic of the Congo, and to use their beautiful and painful history to redesign the future".

It deserves to be seen in full, but if you are more straight to the point, skip to 10'30".


#1

 

It's not a video shared on WeTransfer

It's a WeTransfer video.

The format WePresent by WeTransfer sometimes gives goodies. Today is the turn of Grimes, a self-made musician, whose latest album dates back to 2015.

Passionate about synths and new technologies, and mother to the son of Elon Musk, whose events on the name have thrilled the news.

Her latest single doesn't have an official video, but it has as many as her fans. Because through WeTransfer she provided a sort of Art Kit, complete with her own one shot on the green screen, to allow anyone to make a video at will. Enjoy this interview that talks about music, artificial intelligence, new ways of thinking about music videos and a future à la Ready, Player One.
