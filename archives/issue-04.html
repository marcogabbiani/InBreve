N°4 | 4 May 2020

Hello,

And welcome to a new issue of Finding Beauty, a newsletter powered by Imille. A weekly collection for the curious, the self-motivated learners and the explorers inside us all.
Five absolutely arbitrary doses of content, in the form of an absolutely arbitrary Top 5, once a week, selected by Antonio, our Creative Director and Head of Storytelling.

In this uncertain period, we can only offer one piece of advice that is certain: stay close to beauty. As close as you can. Beauty is the only thing you can stick to without the risk of getting sick.No gloves, masks or gel needed. You can kiss beauty against the doors of the night, to quote Prevert. You can gather with her. You can give her the low and the high five. Then a punch, a caress, a pinch on the cheeks or a tickle behind the ear. Intertwine, embrace, cling to beauty. With her you can do all of this and much much more. And the only virus she will attack you with will be the virus of personal growth and maybe, if you’re lucky, the love bug. So, to paraphrase the little masterpiece that is this movie, Let the beauty do the talking.

[Remember that you can contribute to Finding Beauty too, by pointing out all that you find beautiful on the Internet. Just send an email to findingbeauty@imille.agency]

 

#5

 

Piece of cake, piece of art

We’ve been posting tons of pics of more or less successful “pande-meals". Some of them, even very instagramable. And then, there are recipes that are real pieces of art. Lauren, for instance, is not a pastry chef, but a very talented designer.
On her Instagram profile, she has been sharing with us amazing cakes and apple pies that are a pleasure for the eyes. I can't assure you about the taste and, on the contrary, I've always been wary of such beautiful cakes. But the detailed decorations and the meticulous precision in garnishing them, makes these sweets more suitable for the MoMa than for a pastry shop.

#4

 

I love Berlin

We have already spoken in previous issues of how a photographer can fall in love with his city, to the point of obsessively making it the object of his aesthetic and narrative research. Maybe someone will remember Giovanni Genzini and his vice of endlessly speaking about his beautiful teal and orange Milan.
Today we are talking about a new case of obsessive-compulsive love. That between the photographer Mischa Heuer towards his Berlin, to which he has dedicated, rather than a gallery of shots, a series of love letters. 

Something tells me that the mugs in his house are all marked, "I Love Berlin".
 

Mischa Heuer


#3

 

Stock & Post


Samir Belhamra's diagnosis is different. He is in love with the image too, but in his gallery there is not even a pic. Well, not even a pic taken by him, at least. His experiment lies in exclusively taking stock images and transforming them into a new story, dominated by the same colors.

Proving that one thing is to know the tour of C major. Another is to use it to compose The Beatles’ Yesterday.

#2

 

Social animals

In the fourth century before Christ, a man named Aristotle had hypothesized that we human beings are social animals, tending to gather by nature. So think about how tiring it must be for our DNA helices to come to terms with the lockdown. And in fact, we didn’t just come to terms with it. We overcame it. We gather where we cannot, and wherever we can, we do it to the maximum.

Take for example this incredible concert by Travis Scott on Fortnite, attended by millions of users at the same time, rewriting the history of digital events. Not even if we add Woodstock to Wembley to Pink Floyd in Venice and multiply them together, would we come close to such a number. Funny that it was during a global lockdown that we managed to witness the most crowded concert in history. 

What a curious breed of domestic animal we humans are, aren’t we?


#1

 

David VS Goliath

It's not Hollywood, baby. It's more.
It’s the last video of the Woodkid project, shot by that little monster, Yoann Lemoine, who knows a little thing or two about video clips (take a look here). Although it officially came out on April 24th, it’s clear that the shooting ended long before the beginning of the post-production. From now on, each video clip will seem to you like David against Goliath. Little else to say, a lot to see.

Enjoy these 4 minutes of pure cinema that, in theory, is music.
