Hello,

And welcome to another issue of Finding Beauty, a collection for the curious, the self-motivated learners and the explorers inside us all, edited by Antonio, our Creative Director and Head of Storytelling.

You can read all the previous issues here. 

A child rides his bicycle for the first meter without training wheels.
A piece of cookie remains in the back of the upper arch of your teeth and you move it forward with your tongue. The idea that you are now doing exactly that gesture. You raise your eyes to reflect on the beautiful page of a book. A meme made you laugh so much that you even blew your nose.
While shaving, you realize that you cut yourself in the same place your father always used to. Opening the cutlery drawer, you notice that they are all beautifully aligned. In the park in front of your window, the grass has just been cut and you feel the smell. Your feet touch the clean carpet, as you just step out of the shower. Your daughter makes the same noise with her mouth that you do when focused on something important. A ball rolls along the sidewalk. Two girls kiss each other very loudly on the stairs of a church. The terraces of Rome, as I'm writing this.

There is still wonder in this world.
Still beauty.
And still, 
you won’t see it
until you look.

#5

DENIM ART


You can do endless things with a pair of jeans. You can shorten and turn them into a pair of Daisy Dukes, with pockets that go beyond the frayed hem of the shorts themselves. You can add wear and tear to flavor them with experience and spirit. You can sleep in them. You can make communication masterpieces about them. And last but not least, you can make art with them. 

If you don't believe me, at least believe your eyes and go and see what Ian Berry is doing. And make peace forever with the idea that every piece can be a piece of art.
 


#4

EVERY TIME A SHUTTER CLOSES, A STORY OPENS


A closed shutter. If History was divided into pre-Covid and post-Covid, just like before and after Christ, we could say that there was a time before the virus when lowering the shutter was just the end of a day of work and the beginning of the rest. Or simply the end of the day and the beginning of the night. Then the virus came, some shutters went down and never reopened. Whatever the motivation that moves these horizontal doors up and down, it is the story behind the presses the spring.

And if this were a cinema school, I would advise you to visit this profile and choose a shutter. And try to imagine that story.
 

 


#3

HOW THINGS SHAPE CULTURE


A floppy disk, Kodak's disposable camera, a Burger King napkin, a fan. These are some of the objects preserved in the digital archive Thngs, which since 2016 collects a huge database of everything ever created by humans. Objects that remind us of the past - especially the 80s and 90s - to preserve the memory of what deteriorates, goes out of fashion, breaks.

"We made it easy and convenient to discover, share and keep information about things", explained the co-founder and C.E.O. Dima Dewinn, a Russian designer. "Physical conservation of all things is not possible, but we can keep the information on these. We created Thngs as a Wikipedia for physical objects, where everything has its page with data, images and files, and anyone can modify them. We want to develop a tool to collect things. A Noah's ark for the material world".

Maybe they are just objects. Yet they shaped culture.
 


 


#2

OVER MY SKIN


Skin should be an anatomical fact. 
To be studied at school, and in particular in medical Universities. More or less like we study botany or algebra.

Instead, skin is political. Skin and its colors are bringing people to the streets all over the world. Throughout history it brought them on the holds of ships, in chains, behind gun sights or swords; then on bicycles with square thermal containers, which run straight like rows of cotton or tomatoes.

Skin should be an anatomical fact. Instead, it is political.

And like all things in politics, it pushes us to take a stand. Only a few can afford not to take it. It then became impossible for brands before the consumer court.

These two brands - P&G and Vaseline - have taken their own. With two powerful, polarizing manifestos.

The color of the skin should be an anatomical fact. Instead, it can become poetry.
 


#1

QUESTION EVERYTHING. EAT NOWHERE

 

I wanted to tell you the story and the incredible vision of this brand in my own words, but then I realized that their own were better than any other. The protagonist is called Moa Gürbüzer. "She quit her job and, in 2013, she started Oddbird. Her vision was to question and change the alcohol norms of society by creating world-class wines but liberating them from alcohol". Now, your first reaction will be: "Come on, wine without alcohol, seriously?".

If you are Italian, this will seem to you the equivalent of a sacrilege. But in the Middle Ages, even playing with black cats was a sacrilege and not infrequently led you to the stake. That's why all that is sacred deserves to be questioned. Just like the fact that good wine cannot be without alcohol. 

This is exactly Moa Gürbüzer's approach. On the brand's website, probably one of the best-written I have ever seen, there is their extraordinary section Philosophy, where this passage particularly strikes me: "People and planet are suffering from decades of wastefulness. Time is our most valuable and precious resource. Don’t let anything get in the way between you and your personal time. But don’t forget to have a good time. Make the time to spend time with those you really care about. Don’t waste your time on hungover weekends in bed. It’s time to make a change. Be ahead of your time and be mindful of your drinking habits. We know that society will change and we will be more resourceful with our time, it’s only a matter of time.".

If all this, together with their beautiful product photos, is not enough, in August they will launch an amazing initiative: book a table at nowhere. Only 6 tables, in the middle of nowhere. Book without knowing where you will have dinner, but you'll be sure of two things: it will be something absolutely isolated and it will be something absolutely extraordinary.

Question everything but the beauty.
