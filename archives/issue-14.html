N°14 | 12 July 2020

Hello,

And welcome to another issue of Finding Beauty, a collection for the curious, the self-motivated learners and the explorers inside us all, edited by Antonio, our Creative Director and Head of Storytelling. 
[If you want to contribute, point out all that you find beautiful on the Internet, by sending an email to findingbeauty@imille.agency]

You can read all the previous issues here. 

If I wanted to look for the common thread that links this issue, I would say that the fil rouge is silence.
The silence of a man who could leave the scene with a triumphant march and instead does it quietly, without anyone noticing. The silence of a packaging from which everything is removed and reduced to its very essence. The silence that still surrounds topics related to the bodies of women, which seem to be barely addressed.

In the movie Life is Beautiful, Roberto Benigni enjoys asking riddles to a future Nazi hierarch, and among them there is a very beautiful one: “if you mention its name, it no longer exists”. The answer is, precisely, silence. Let's try to rediscover what perfume can give our lives a few drops of silence. Let's try for a moment not to even name it. After all, to listen to something or someone, silence is a necessary condition.

Maybe a little silence will allow us every now and then to listen to the beauty a little more.
 


#5

WHEN THE MUSIC GOES SILENTLY

 

"I do not want to bother". 

This is the sentence that strikes me the most in the farewell letter that Ennio Morricone left before his death. The man who wrote the soundtracks of our lives chooses the most silent fade out. He chooses to leave the scene quietly, on tiptoe, without even taking the last standing ovation. He chooses the understatement, the absence of clamor. After harnessing and combining each type of sound, he finally reminds us of the value of silence. Making it impossible not to listen to that too.

After many lessons in cinema and music, the Maestro gives us all one last, beautiful lesson: that of humility. In a world where everyone is making an effort to be seen by using the loudest voice possible, he whispers his last greeting in our ears.

Beauty or poetry is often sought in the great themes and through the great metaphors. Instead, beauty lurks between our toes, in the water drains of the French Riviera, where ici commence la mer is written instead of forbidden to throw waste.

Or in a printed, wrinkled and badly photographed letter.
Here’s to you, Maestro.
 


#4

NOTHING IS MORE NECESSARY THAN THE UNNECESSARY


If the beauty explorer in you often takes you on social media, you have probably already met one of the renderings of Kunel Gaur, an Indian designer who has reinterpreted some of the most famous packaging in a minimal way.

These are mostly food and consumer products whose success also depends on their iconic packaging (see Pringles, Coca Cola and Kinder). Gaur has redesigned them all by eliminating colors, photos and graphic effects and reducing everything to a white background with black elements and texts strictly in Helvetica.
The effect is depersonalizing, as much as it is fascinating. How much does the container affect the choice of content? How important is the part for the whole? We will probably never know exactly.

But we know that a couple of centuries ago, even before many of these consumer products existed, Oscar Wilde wrote that nothing is more necessary than the unnecessary. And maybe it's enough to know.
 


 


#3

ADULTS WELCOME


In addition to being the title of the so-called "song of the century", Imagine is also the line of this Cannes Grand Prix press from several years ago. 

Those who have children know that the best toys are not toys. That you can buy the most beautiful and expensive toy, but an empty pack of pasta and some markers will surpass its charm. Imagination is the most beautiful game of all. Even great writers or great directors know this when they leave empty spaces, as Umberto Eco defined them, which will be filled by the reader’s imagination. If in Tarantino's Reservoir Dogs, a film about a bank robbery, we never see the bank robbery, we instead spend time wondering what went wrong during the robbery.

Lego knows this too. They know that imagination has no age and for this reason they launched “Lego Art”, that is, the opportunity to have fun rebuilding the classics.

The time has come for adults not only to be buyers, but also end consumers.
 


#2

IDEAS NOT SO LIDL


Stylists and creative directors, prototypes and production lines, trials and focus groups, billions of dollars of investments in communication, influencer strategy, films made with director-stars, like the last of DIOR with Matteo Garrone.

And then comes Lidl. Yes, THAT Lidl; the one that sells the products directly inside the cardboard boxes; the discount store with Finkbrau beer perpetually on offer (assuming that what's inside the cans really is beer). Well, that Lidl launched its own model of sneakers, with a Lidl price of 12.99 euros. And they become an incredible success, so much so that they can be resold for upwards of 450 pounds. What does it have to do with silence? Where everyone strives to make the show bigger, more newsworthy, to hire the most famous testimonials and the most expensive productions, by adding a price tag to a pair of shoes worth triple their real value, those of Lidl manage to obtain an unpredictable commercial success, without noise.

A story halfway between the serious and the facetious, which can be a lesson for the industry: perhaps we should really start striving for a little less big and a little more Lidl.
 


 


#1

WOMBSTORIES


The role that the Libresse / Bodyform brand is playing in freeing the global conversation from stereotypes and taboos related to the theme of menstruation (and the way it is represented in communication), is an increasingly key role. And it is a role that has earned thanks to very solid strategies and extraordinary advertising pieces.

Their latest work is an authentic and even raw story of the world of women, but not without an imaginative and brilliant level of irony. I would not have thought that talking about menstruation could become a show. But maybe, that's the problem.

Maybe it is time for this topic to come out, once and for all. Out of deafening silence.
