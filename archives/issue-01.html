N°1 | 13 April 2020

Hello,
and welcome to the very first issue of Finding Beauty, a newsletter powered by Imille.

We know that we’re all going through an extraordinary time of our lives, where many things are changing and many others are expected to change. We’re sure you'll agree with us if we say that now, more than ever, there is a great need for beauty in the world.

That’s where the idea of Finding Beauty comes from: a weekly collection for the curious, the self-motivated learners and the explorers inside us all. For those who, whether consciously or not, embrace the kaizen (literally, “change for better”) culture.
A treasure trove of the beautiful things we collect and cherish throughout the workweek — and over the weekend, too. An attempt to help all of us become better professionals and why not, better people.

How Finding Beauty works: five absolutely arbitrary doses of contents, in the form of an absolutely arbitrary Top 5, once a week, selected by Imille's Creative Director and Head of Storytelling.
And hey, you can contribute too, by pointing out all that you find beautiful on the Internet. Just drop the beauty via email to findingbeauty@imille.agency.

Shall we begin?

 

#5

 

Quarantine pastime

The media are just counting the number of infected and victims in Milan. We prefer to look at the countless pieces of art in the city, with this nice solution that also helps you to survive, in a creative way, during this hopefully unrepeatable period.
 

milanoartguide


#4

 

Stykka to the plan

One thing is Working From Home every now and then, another is when the home becomes your workplace itself. At home, for instance, you realize that the chair you have on average sat in for the 30-minutes-dinner becomes an Iron Maiden when you’re having to tolerate your tush in it for hours.
That being said, we were fascinated by this idea of a "cardboard design", which introduces recycling, economically and environmentally sustainable issues, as well as potentially interesting applications (think of those people who are forced to live on the street in broken down and weathered boxes). Stykka is its name and it sounds like a plan if don’t have a proper workstation at home.
 

stykka.com


#3

 

From couch potatoes to couch potatriots

Perhaps the day will come when Burger King will miss a beat. But not today.
The question is: do we have necessarily speak about Covid-19 in a rhetorical emotional piano-tracked ads, or can we joke about that rhetoric itself? The answer from BK is a lesson of copywriting, lateral thinking and execution. By the way, how did they produce this film during the lockdown? Maybe in the good old USA they are not so restrictive with productions?
Anyway, they have made it with the courage to bring irony to one of the most sacred things in Trumpist America: patriotism.


#2

 

We can be heroes just for one month

An Instagram account that collects cute illustrations of those figures who, despite themselves, became heroic in the dawn of the Coronavirus era.

 
coronafighterz


#1

 

Streaming Emotions

Among the abundant streamable contents available these days, with a little effort something beautiful can be found. For example, the Paris Philharmonic that plays Ravel's Bolero, this beautiful composition that never develops, like a very long intro of something that doesn't get anywhere, but that gives you a sense of harmony with the world.
Whenever we are in front of a piece of art that we don't have enough cultural tools to judge, we judge it with the simplest tool: emotions.
Now try to judge this one.
