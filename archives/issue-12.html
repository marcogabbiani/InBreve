10 beautiful things I discovered this week:

1) The pressure of the mask straps behind the ears gives me a headache, but it makes it even better when I massage those points with with my index fingers;

2) The doorman at my house is not from Seychelles as I thought, but rather from the Maldives, and this explains the scent of fried bananas you can smell near his house. Maldivian people go crazy for french fries and deep-fried bananas. He calls himself Jack - because his real name would be too difficult to pronounce - and says he got it from Titanic. But in Titanic, isn't Jack the one who dies?

3) A few days ago, while entering a laundromat, I caught the two owners who came out of the back clearly disheveled. They were indulging themselves in "the supreme ecstasy of love's perfection", to quote a famous Italian song. They are married, and it is amazing to think that passion seized them on the ironing board;

4) My granddaughter Matilde is a nerd and she had her first report card where the lowest grade was 9. Her favorite subject is English. He asked for a used bike as a gift, because she is growing, so it is not worth buying a new one. My granddaughter Matilde is 6 years old;

5) If you rotate an iPhone, turn on the camera in slowmo mode and put the focus very close to a flower, you can get a cinematic sequence on the details of the petals;

6) The length from your wrist to your elbow is exactly equal to that of your foot (and now twist yourself to try to see if it's true);

7) The zero Tzaziki exists and it comes without garlic and with a more delicate taste;

8) Emily Rataioschi (I could never spell this correctly) went blond, but she was better before;

9) The light in my house is the best in the hours when I was usually not there, because I was at the office;

10) You can make a mojito with basil and a cherry tomato, instead of mint and lime. I thought that it would be terrible, but it is actually very good. It is called a Mediterranean Mojito.

So what? So it’s incredible where the search for beauty can take you, if you pay attention to beauty. And even if sometimes beauty seems to forget about you, don't forget about beauty. 
 


#5

Why choose between a good book or a good movie when you can have both? Matt Stevens is an illustrator who draws a lot of nice stuff. This one - of the films reimagined as old book covers - is certainly the nicest.
I envision some of my favorite films as vintage books. Not a best of list, just movies I love, he said.

#4

THE BOUNCING MAN


You may have seen him challenging the laws of physics and the rigidity of bodies in this Apple masterpiece but Yoann Bourgeois is much more than a TV commercial actor.
On Instagram he defines himself as "artiste de cirque", but actually he is a performer who does incredible things.
Scrolling through his profile gives a sense of fulfillment and perfection of the world. And maybe that's what we really need.
 


#3

PORNHUB PREMIUM LOVERS


Cultural heritage: watching porn is a wrong, "dirty" thing, to be kept hidden, instead of an incredible form of entertainment, experimentation and awareness of one's body at any age, especially in puberty. 

The data: couples who watch porn together tend to have a more fulfilling sex life than those who watch it separately. 

The experiment: how much do you really know your partner's tastes? In this new story by Pornhub - whose global work in communication is helping to reduce the cultural heritage we talked about at the beginning - some couples are watching porn content together for the first time. And they are also compensated by a final reward. 

Oh, wait, that's not what you think.
 


 


#2

TIK-TOK TIK-TOK: THE TIME FOR BRANDS HAS ARRIVED 


TikTok makes me think of these old Marmite campaigns. Either you love it or you hate it. Whether you love it or hate it, if you work in communication you have no choice: you have to be there.
The areas of improvement of the algorithm are still many (if you accidentally saw a video with a dog for two seconds, you will only have videos with dogs for the next 10 years) there is still much to do also on the possibilities of planning and usable formats. 

But it’s becoming more and more interesting, because it goes from the most nonsense challenges, to being the main channel used during the Minneapolis protests, precisely because of its versatility of editing and storytelling. 

The creative possibilities are many and still little explored by brands.
I really liked these two examples. On one side the Uffizi Gallery in Florence, on the other, of course, Burger King. On one side, one of the oldest cultural institutions is searching for its own language on one of the most recent social networks, on the other a brand that is using it to convey its positioning, have it your way.

Keep an eye on it and don't be rushed with the reviews. Very soon it will be impossible to ignore it.
 


#1

PARTERRE DE BOIS

 

What would you think if I told you that tomatoes that listen to a particular melody can grow two and a half times more than untreated ones?
Yes, music is good for plants, and decades of studies all over the world prove it. Initially, official science looked at this hypothesis with skepticism, but over time they understood that plant organisms respond to musical stimuli. It is no coincidence that in the past and in oldest traditions, agricultural rituals were almost always characterized by songs and music, to propitiate the seasons, to sprout, grow and collect plants. And, obviously, to ease the effort of manual labor. Think of the blues, for example.

But all this is not enough to explain the extraordinary performance of the Barcelona Opera, where a string quartet performed, for the first time in history, in front of a parterre composed only of stems, leaves, soil, pots, plants.
Eugenio Ampudia, the artist who came up with the performance, describes it in this way: “At a time when most of humanity is locked up in closed spaces and was forced not to move, nature has crept in to occupy the spaces that we have given up. And it has done so at its own pace, according to its patient biological cycle. Can we broaden our empathy and share it with other species? Let's start using art and music, inviting nature to a large concert hall.”

Who knows, maybe music will set the tone to return in harmony with the world.
