Hello,

This is another issue of Finding Beauty, a collection for the curious, the self-motivated learners and the explorers inside us all, edited by Antonio, our Creative Director and Head of Storytelling. 

If you want to contribute, point out all that you find beautiful on the Internet, by sending an email to findingbeauty@imille.agency. And remember, you can read all the previous issues here. 

Welcome back, beauty seekers!

As we have been saying during our explorations into beauty, September is the real New Year's Eve. The most beautiful one.

Without the firecrackers that scare the dogs (and that force us, advertisers, to find indispensable solutions), without the red underwear or the lentils eaten with your hands and the other unmissable "elements of folklore". And above all, without the social pressure of having to do something special and expensive.

And like every new year, we look to the next one full of expectations, projects, promises.

Some of the content you will find in this new Top 5 are in fact promises of beauty. Some already kept, others that will be revealed shortly. The most pessimistic say that 2020 was just the trailer for 2021. So, whatever this "new year" will look like, promise yourself some beauty.

Just as, for an arid field, a breath of wind is the promise of the comfort of rain. After all, it is what you will really need.
 

#5
WORKING HAS NEVER BEEN SO SMART


You are in bed and cannot sleep.

Your body is tired, but your brain continues to marvel at how differently each c is pronounced in "Pacific Ocean". You've finished the first season of Cobra Kai's nostalgia operation, read a few pages of that compelling book about the Civil War, and even finished a round of videos about Polynesians building a Jacuzzi inside a clay well on YouTube, when you suddenly come across a video with a promising title: I prerecorded myself in video meetings for a week (and nobody knew).

Although the video has an unacceptable length in the TikTok or Triller era - FYI: in the US, a boomer invasion and widespread fear of Trump banning the Chinese social network is causing many TikTok teens to seek solace in the rival app, Triller - this video experiment is actually interesting and makes viewers think a bit. It makes us think about the quality and efficiency of video meetings that, like it or not, will play a significant role in the "new normal" and the theoretical (but not only) possibility of replacing ourselves with someone or something else. Possibly more effective than even Homer Simpson's typing bird.

But don't worry: you will always be irreplaceable for your team.
 


 

#4
I WISH YOU WOULDN'T STOP, CHARLIE


Charlie Kaufman is thinking of ending this.

Instead, I would like him to never finish writing and shooting. For those who don't know who this gentleman is, I'll just name a few titles: Eternal sunshine of a Spotless Mind (for which he won an Oscar), Being John Malkovich or the unrivaled Adaptation. They are all scripts born from his mind and his pen, but the list would be longer than that.

A few years ago he started as a director, making beautiful films like Anomalisa. His latest movie, which I linked to at the beginning, recently came out on Netflix and looks very promising.

Enjoy this weekend and let me know if the promise has been kept.
 

#3
STATTOO


The images of his statues that seamlessly cross Michelangelo, Canova and Young Signorino, have depopulated on social networks this summer of national holidays. Fabio Viale, sculptor who filled Pietrasanta with beautiful pieces, blends the art of marble with that of tattooing. "My goal is not to bring the present into the past, but to make the past available to the present, to create a generational bridge".

Nothing more to add, your honor.
 


 

#2
GOODBYE FILMS, WELCOME CINEMATIC ACTIONS


Whatever happens to the French artist JR, it turns out to be a small masterpiece. 

Imagine what can happen if he crosses the talented Alice Rohrwacher, director of beautiful short and long films like that little pearl of Happy as Lazzaro. It happens, therefore, that the two have given life to something that we mere mortals would call "auteur films" and which instead they prefer to place in a category that can be defined as "cinematic action", that is, something halfway between contemporary art performance and his ability to embellish it with beautiful shots. This is a promise of beauty too, revealed at the 77th Venice International Film Festival.

But if it were even remotely comparable to the beautiful Faces Places, well, it would still be a masterpiece.


 

#1
FKA SPLITS


Still, in the context of what we could define visual performances, more than just films, there is this new video by FKA Twigs. You could say a lot about her, but basically there is one that you can’t miss: she is really cool.

Everything she does has an incredible level of visual and sound research. After Spike Jonze's film for Apple - impossible not to have seen for those who work in the ad-land - she is back with this spectacular work in which she literally splits in half. (The director is Hiro Murai, the same of This is America by Childish Gambino, Guava Island, etc. ).

Because the truth is that it would take two of her.
 


 

Happy New Year!
