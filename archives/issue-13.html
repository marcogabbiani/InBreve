This newsletter will forever be in debt to the world of art, from which it greedily takes inspiration from, exhibiting the same restraint (or lack thereof) as a yoyo dieter with an empty belly faced with a fresh plate of pasta carbonara.

But this new issue, in particular, owes more to art more than the previous ones. The same sector that is considered "not essential", in which those "artists who give us so much fun" work (to use two known and unhappy quotes from these past months).

After all, can we do our job without drinking from the source of the open bar of endless inspiration which is art? So, take a drink or two. There are no queues to wait in. No drink cards to collect. You can have all the cocktails you can carry or take a long one and sip it a little at a time.

Have a toast to the beauty, my friends.
 


#5

THE WEBSITE THAT TURNS SHOPPING INTO ART


A few years ago I happened to be in an email exchange in which Kanye West's agent asked for 850 thousand dollars for the use of his beautiful No Church In The Wild in a tv commercial. Beautiful piece, with a masterful video clip, shot by that infinite master of cinema who is Romain Gavras, author of things like this or this. And this is where my closest level of contact with Kanye West begins and will end forever. 

For everything else, there is the contact I have had with his art, which in recent years has never stopped producing always very cool outputs, from both a musical and visual point of view.
His latest work will be the launch of an e-commerce platform, which promises to be an experience closer to art than to shopping.

Dive into this Fast Company story that explains the inspirations, technologies and the type of experience that this site promises to give.

Maybe your credit card will be the next point of contact with him.
 

 


#4

PROBABLY THE BEST MUSEUM EVER


Alan Gibbs is one of New Zealand's richest men. Successful entrepreneur, he founded Gibbs Amphibians, specialized in the production of high-speed amphibious vehicles. Described this way, he seems to be a man of all crawlers and engines. Instead he is a passionate collector.

20 years ago Gibbs created Gibbs Farm, an area of about 4 km located in one of the two major islands that make up New Zealand. Here, in a natural context of extraordinary charm, the great sculptures of 22 world-renowned artists stand among the most influential names on the contemporary scene. A park immersed in tranquility, between bodies of water lapped by crystalline light, grazing flocks and emerald handkerchiefs of vegetation: the works of art are solitary, alien monuments, designed in perfect communication with the context. Becoming a harmonic part of it. One of the most interesting open-air contemporary art galleries. 

Just a shame that the open-air distance to get there is not very short.
 


#3

THE LIQUESCENCE OF BODIES


In physics, the transitions from one state to another can take several definitions. Specifically, when we go from a solid-state to a liquid one, we speak about fusion. Ice, heat, water. 

But when something that our brain hardly catalogs melts away, alienation effects are generated. In short, it is in those passages of matter that art is generated. Dalì proved it with his watches, but this time we talk about Faig Ahmed, an artist from Azerbaijan who tampers with and digitally redesigns traditional carpets from Central Asia; then he asks local craftsmen to make them on traditional frames, creating objects in which the sign is continuously moved, pixelated, liquefied. As Ahmed himself said: "I love being hostage to tradition: it is a test I have to undergo to feel free."

So, be the laws of physics, a few centuries of tradition or anything else that stands between you and your goal, be grateful for your obstacles, for they are the very things that will set you free.
 


 


#2

THIS MUST BE HIS PLACE


According to a cliché, it’s the shortness of time, pain and human suffering that are the greatest authors of all time. Throughout history, we have attributed all sorts of etiquette to these feelings: nausea, unhappiness, spite, cosmic pessimism, realism and so on.

If this thesis is true, then the suffering that Covid-19 has generated can only have produced a great deal of art.
Just like the one that pushed Sartre, Leopardi or Verga during their time. 

Whatever the truth that lies behind this cliché may be, this performance by Angelo Bonello inspired by the global lockdown was hypnotic for me. A critic described it this way:

The condition of reclusion experienced by humans during COVID time led Bonello to create “This is my place”. The thought underlying this action arises from an atavistic question: what is the place in which and the way how a man should live? The equilateral triangle, the Platonic form par excellence, origin of many complex forms in nature, thus divides the space, the house, the place where to take refuge into (nature), the place from which to relegate civilization out. The performance took place into this ephemeral and inviolable space, which can be set up both into nature or in urban spaces such as squares or parks, and it’s opened to the Audience who walks, sleeps, reflects, stands and gets out in perpetual motion, as a metaphor of always moving forward and never stopping. One of the triangle’s vertices has been accurately aligned with the sunset point to make the Artwork claim the function of an environmental compass showing the way to eternity, marked by the cycle sunrise - sunset/day - night, as an absolute balance between nature and progress.

I will just say this: for some reason, I can’t stop watching it.
 


#1

SINGING THE "KUNG FLU" 


Sascha. Baron. Cohen. If these three words were a rhetorical figure, they would be an ascending climax of genius. One of those artists of such incredible versatility, to succeed in comic roles as much as in dramatic ones; in the Apollonian, as much as in the Dionysian; in being an interpreter, as much as an author; in being Borat, as much as the histrionic protagonist of the beautiful SPY. 

His career is underpinned by ingenious pranks, such as the one in which he pretended to break the historic stick of Charlie Chaplin and almost kill the last survivor of his cinema. Today he has outdone himself again, surprising us with a new beautiful stunt: pretending to be a country singer and trolling all the participants of an American far-right rally, whose IQ seems so low as not to distinguish, in fact, a joke from reality.

CNN is spreading fake news, 
they're controlled by you know who's, 
CNN what we gonna do? 
Inject them with the Wuhan Flu!

What more can I say? I love this guy.
