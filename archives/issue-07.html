Hello,

And welcome to another issue of Finding Beauty, a weekly collection for the curious, the self-motivated learners and the explorers inside us all, edited by Antonio, our Creative Director and Head of Storytelling.

These unlock-downs, which will hopefully be gradual and conscientious, are leaving me with a great desire to take back the spring that was stolen from us, while we were closed at home to become audience or authors of any and every type of streaming. From yoga lessons to recipes with puff pastry and vegan cooked pears (not to be confused with carnivorous pears, of course). I really hope this will be a new spring for everyone. May it be the spring of beauty.

So collect the flowers of beauty without hesitation. Remove them from the stem or petals or uproot them with all the soil. Create memorable bouquets of beauty. Just make sure that the search for it never withers.

It's springtime for beauty, if you want it, baby.

The book was much better

For the first time, here you have not movies taken from books but books taken from movies. And so it is resolved the long-standing dispute between "the book was better" VS "the film was better", where in most cases, the latter succumbs to the former.

Fades in black, music crescendo, end credits with the chief toolmaker always put at the end, yet God only knows how much he did for the movie.

#4


Should we turn off the advertisement?

The philosopher says that some things exist as a function of their absence. This may seem like nonsense, but think about it for a moment: you only notice electricity when the light goes off. In the same way, you realized the importance of the people you love or of your freedom, only when a global pandemic denied you access to them for a while. Things that have returned to having value, thanks to the lack of them.

Coming to the adv, for years the competition was supposed to be about who screamed louder, who put the biggest logo, the packaging in the highest position, with the lowest price, leading people to develop the same listening level as the Bose headphones that eliminate external noise: zero.

And so, we have become the laughing stock of society. 

Maybe today we are starting to abandon the "interrupting model" - the ads that interrupts the film we are watching, the website we are scrolling, the playlist we are listening to or the landscape we are looking at - to move on to a more dialoguing, cultural, native model. Meanwhile, it was necessary to "turn off" the advertisement so that a photographer noticed it and gave us back a little white noise.

The same used to relax or to help children sleep.
 


#3

 

Dance like everyone is watching

The ballet? Boring, isn't it?
But have you ever gone to the ballet?

What I consider the most important exercise to stimulate our creative side is called "do the bad thing". That is, basically, imposing yourself to do something you don't like, that makes you sick, in order to get out of your comfort zone and make the most beautiful and interesting discoveries. To stimulate the serendipity. So every now and then, "do the bad thing."

Order Thai, if you hate it, and taste it. Read poetry or an essay, if you only usually are into novels. And if this thesis has convinced you, try now to watch ballet. At least the 3 minutes of this video.

And then tell me if, after looking at this Honk Kong company dancing as if the world had eyes only for them, you expected that ballet could be such a thing.
 


#2

 

The new wave of outdoor?

What if this was all for advertising to get noticed again? Whenever we watch films showing the "advertisement in the future", the outdoors are presented through holograms, smell-o-vision, retinal scans, etc (think of Blade Runner, Ghost in the shell or Minority Report).
Using a cliché, reality will probably end up overcoming fantasy. But in the meantime, let's look at what is already possible: putting the wave of an ocean inside a building. What is the future of the outdoors?

Who cares. Just immerse yourself in this fascinating present.


#1

 

Listening is everything

When data becomes an insight. That insight becomes an idea. An idea that takes off. Ahhh, how nice when everything is in its place. When "everything is illuminated".

  • The data: every time we listen to a song on Spotify there is at least one other person in the world who is listening to that same song.
  • The insight: we are never alone even when we think we are. And perhaps listening to the same thing also means that we could listen more to each other.
  • The idea: a site where you can see in real-time who is listening to your own song in the world, just at that moment.
The execution: judge it yourself.
I only judge the fact that in a world where everyone is data-driven, fortunately there are those who really are. Giving us beautiful pieces of creativity.
